The pattern repeats

Welcome. This is where I'll think out loud.

Here's the thought I want to start with. Before computers were cheap, computer was a job title — a person, usually a room full of people, doing arithmetic by hand. When the machines arrived they were rare and absurdly expensive, so you didn't waste them. Time on a mainframe was rationed. You batched your work, handed only the hard problems to the machine, and did the rest yourself. Plenty of smart people looked at that and concluded compute would always be scarce — something you borrowed carefully, never something you spilled.

They were wrong, but not because they misjudged the machine. They were wrong because spending all that money on scarce compute is exactly what bought the abundance that came after. The expensive version had to exist first.

Agents and AI are sitting in that same room right now. Tokens cost money. A capable agent is something you ration, something you hand the hard problems to while you do the cheap parts yourself. It feels like a ceiling. It's a floor.

So that's the lens I'll be writing through — not magic, not hype, just a pattern that's run before and is running again. If it costs too much or runs too slow today, that's not the end of the story. It's the part where someone decides it's worth paying for anyway.

Glad you're here. Pull up a chair.
